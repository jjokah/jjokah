## From Possibility to Practice

The danger with AI is thinking about it in the abstract. "AI will transform everything" is both true and useless. What is actually useful is knowing: *In my industry, in my role, for this specific task: what does AI do well, and what tool should I use?*

This guide answers that question across six industries and then maps the major tool categories to the cognitive tasks they serve best.

## AI Across Industries

### Healthcare and Life Sciences

Healthcare AI is applied at two levels: clinical and administrative. The administrative applications are more immediately accessible to most professionals.

**Clinical applications:**
- **Medical imaging**: AI systems (like Google DeepMind's AlphaFold for proteins, or Viz.ai for stroke detection) identify abnormalities in X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans with accuracy matching or exceeding specialists
- **Drug discovery**: AI accelerates the identification of candidate molecules for new drugs from months to days
- **Predictive diagnostics**: Models trained on patient records flag high-risk patients before symptoms escalate

**Administrative applications (accessible now):**
- **Clinical note generation**: Ambient AI tools (Suki, Nuance DAX) listen to doctor-patient conversations and write structured clinical notes automatically, saving physicians 2–3 hours per day
- **Prior authorization**: AI drafts insurance pre-authorization requests from clinical notes
- **Patient communication**: AI drafts personalized follow-up messages, appointment reminders, and care instructions

> **Key Insight:** In healthcare, the highest-value AI applications reduce administrative burden so clinicians can spend more time on the work that actually requires their expertise.

### Finance and Banking

Finance was an early adopter of algorithmic systems. Modern AI extends this tradition into language and reasoning tasks.

**Risk and compliance:**
- **Fraud detection**: Real-time transaction scoring flags anomalous patterns; AI models trained on millions of transactions catch fraud that rule-based systems miss
- **Anti-money-laundering (AML)**: AI analyzes complex transaction networks to identify suspicious patterns across accounts
- **Credit risk modeling**: ML models augment or replace traditional credit scoring with more predictive signals

**Investment and analysis:**
- **Earnings call analysis**: LLMs extract key signals from earnings calls and investor reports at scale
- **Portfolio commentary**: AI drafts personalized quarterly portfolio reports for wealth management clients
- **Market research synthesis**: Analysts use AI to synthesize hundreds of pages of reports into actionable briefs

**Accessible applications:**
- **Excel/spreadsheet analysis**: AI analyzes uploaded spreadsheets and answers questions in plain English
- **Contract review**: AI flags unusual clauses or deviations from standard terms in legal and financial agreements
- **Customer service**: AI handles routine account inquiries, freeing advisors for complex client relationships

### Education

Education AI spans both the experience of learning and the work of educators.

**For educators:**
- **Lesson plan generation**: AI drafts differentiated lesson plans for different learning levels from a single topic description
- **Assessment creation**: Quizzes, rubrics, and discussion questions generated from source material in seconds
- **Feedback at scale**: AI provides structured formative feedback on student writing, identifying patterns the instructor can address in class
- **Administrative drafting**: Parent communications, progress reports, and IEP documentation drafted in minutes

**For learners:**
- **Personalized tutoring**: AI tutors (Khan Academy's Khanmigo, Socratic by Google) adapt explanations to the learner's level and pace
- **Research assistance**: Students use AI to find, summarize, and organize sources for projects
- **Writing support**: AI helps with brainstorming, outlining, and editing (raising important questions about academic integrity that institutions are actively addressing)

> **Warning:** Institutional policies on AI use in academic work vary widely and are evolving rapidly. Always check your institution's guidelines before using AI assistance in graded work.

### Legal

Legal AI is among the most consequential and requires the highest standards of verification.

**Document review and discovery:**
- AI reviews thousands of documents in discovery far faster than human paralegals, flagging relevant documents and identifying patterns
- Contract analysis tools (Harvey, Ironclad AI, Kira) extract and compare key provisions across large contract portfolios

**Research and drafting:**
- **Legal research**: AI summarizes case law, statutes, and regulations, surfacing relevant precedents; outputs must always be verified by a licensed attorney
- **Brief and memo drafting**: AI drafts initial versions of legal documents from structured prompts; human review and refinement is non-negotiable
- **Contract drafting**: Standard agreements (NDAs, service agreements) drafted from templates in minutes

> **Warning:** The "hallucinated citation" problem is especially dangerous in legal work. AI has fabricated case citations that appeared in court filings, leading to sanctions. Every AI-generated legal citation must be independently verified before use.

**Client communication:**
- Intake questionnaires, status update emails, and FAQ responses drafted and personalized at scale

### Marketing and Creative

Marketing is one of AI's strongest early use cases because it thrives on volume, variation, and iteration.

**Content production:**
- **Copywriting at scale**: Product descriptions, ad headlines, social captions, and email subject lines generated in bulk and A/B tested
- **Content repurposing**: One long-form article transformed into a newsletter, LinkedIn post, Twitter thread, and short video script simultaneously
- **Personalization**: Email campaigns personalized by segment, lifecycle stage, or individual behavior

**Visual and multimedia:**
- **Brand imagery**: Custom illustrations, hero images, and product mockups generated without a designer or stock photo license
- **Ad creative**: Multiple visual variations of ads for different placements and audiences, tested rapidly
- **Video ads**: Short promotional videos assembled from AI-generated visuals, text overlays, and AI voiceover

**Strategy and analysis:**
- **Competitive analysis**: AI synthesizes public information about competitors into structured SWOT frameworks
- **Customer feedback synthesis**: Survey responses, reviews, and support tickets analyzed to surface themes and sentiment patterns

### Software Engineering and Technology

Covered extensively in Module 4, but briefly: AI has transformed how software is written, tested, and maintained.

- **Code completion**: AI completes functions, suggests variable names, and catches syntax errors in real-time (GitHub Copilot, Cursor)
- **Code generation from descriptions**: Developers describe what a function should do in plain English; AI writes the implementation
- **Test generation**: Unit tests, integration tests, and edge case scenarios generated automatically
- **Documentation**: Function docstrings, README files, and API documentation drafted from code
- **Security scanning**: AI identifies vulnerability patterns in code reviews
- **Debugging**: AI analyzes error messages and stack traces to suggest root causes and fixes

## AI Tool Categories

Understanding which tool category serves which cognitive function prevents you from reaching for the wrong tool.

| Tool Category | Primary Function | Best For | Leading Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LLM Chatbots** | Conversational text generation and reasoning | Drafting, summarizing, brainstorming, explaining | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini |
| **AI Writing Assistants** | Writing enhancement and generation in context | Polishing, editing, tone adjustment within documents | Grammarly, Notion AI, Jasper |
| **Image Generators** | Creating visual content from text descriptions | Marketing visuals, illustrations, prototypes | Midjourney, DALL-E 3, Adobe Firefly |
| **Code Assistants** | AI-powered programming help in the IDE | Code completion, generation, debugging | GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Codeium |
| **AI Research Tools** | Information synthesis and citation | Academic research, market analysis | Perplexity AI, Elicit, Consensus |
| **Data Analysis AI** | Querying and interpreting structured data | Spreadsheet analysis, business intelligence | ChatGPT Code Interpreter, Julius |
| **Audio/Voice AI** | Text-to-speech, voice cloning, music generation | Voiceovers, podcasting, accessibility | ElevenLabs, Murf, Suno |
| **Video AI** | Text-to-video, video editing | Marketing video, training content | Runway, Pika, HeyGen |
| **Workflow AI** | Process automation and integration | Connecting apps, automating repetitive tasks | Zapier AI, n8n, Make |

## Choosing the Right Tool: A Decision Framework

Before reaching for an AI tool, answer three questions:

**1. What type of output do I need?**
Text → LLM chatbot or writing assistant  
Image → image generator  
Audio → voice AI  
Video → video AI  
Code → code assistant  
Data insight → data analysis AI  
Automated process → workflow AI  

**2. How sensitive is the information involved?**
If it includes confidential data, PII, or proprietary information, use only enterprise-approved tools with appropriate data handling policies.

**3. How high are the stakes of an error?**
Low stakes (internal draft, brainstorm) → AI output usable with light review  
Medium stakes (external communication, client-facing) → thorough human review required  
High stakes (legal, medical, financial decisions) → AI as research aid only; expert human judgment required for final output

## Hands-On Practice

### Exercise 1: Industry Mapping

Think of your current role and industry. List five specific tasks you perform regularly. For each one:

1. Identify the task type (drafting, analysis, research, communication, coding, visual)
2. Identify the appropriate AI tool category from the table above
3. Rate the sensitivity (low/medium/high) of the data involved
4. Rate the stakes of an error (low/medium/high)

Which tasks are clear candidates for AI assistance right now? Which require more caution?

---

### Exercise 2: Use AI to Analyze a Competitive Landscape

Choose a company in your industry (a competitor or a leader in your field). Use an LLM chatbot to:

> *"Act as a market research analyst. Using only publicly available information, create a SWOT analysis of [Company Name] in the [Industry] space. Structure it as a four-quadrant table."*

Evaluate: How accurate is it based on your own knowledge? What did the AI miss or misrepresent? What would you need to verify independently?

---

### Exercise 3: Content Repurposing Workflow

Find a long article (yours or any published piece). Use an LLM to:

1. Summarize it in three bullet points
2. Rewrite it as a LinkedIn post (under 200 words, professional tone)
3. Extract three discussion questions for a team meeting
4. Draft a tweet thread (5 tweets) hitting the key ideas

How much time did this take compared to doing each manually? What needed refinement?
